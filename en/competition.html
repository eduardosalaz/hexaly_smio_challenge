Competition Format

Two tracks, one leaderboard, and a clear path to CSMIO 2026 in Monterrey.

Tracks

The challenge features two tracks. All participants in both tracks must hold a current SMIO membership. Through the ALIO alliance, members of other Latin American OR societies can obtain SMIO membership at a discounted rate.

Undergraduate Track

For Students

Teams of up to 3 current undergraduate students plus 1 guide (defined as anyone who is not a current undergraduate student — e.g., a graduate student, professor, or industry professional).

This track aligns with SMIO's educational mission and encourages mentorship in the OR community.

Open Track

For Everyone

Teams of up to 3 participants, no restrictions on profile. Open to students, researchers, and industry professionals.

This track maximizes the quality of solutions and opens the competition to the broader OR community.

Scoring System

Hexaly Baseline

Before the competition opens, Hexaly Optimizer runs on all 30 instances to produce a baseline solution for each one. These baselines are published as a reference but do not participate in the leaderboard. The leaderboard tracks only solutions submitted by participant teams.

At the end of the competition, results are presented alongside the Hexaly baseline, providing a clear comparison for each instance. This preserves the narrative of the community benchmarking against a strong industrial solver while ensuring the competition always has a well-defined winner.

Lead-Time Scoring

The scoring system rewards teams that find good solutions early and hold them. For each instance, the team holding the best participant solution accumulates points for every calendar day they maintain it. A bonus is awarded for holding the best solution at the close of the competition.

Score = (days holding the best solution across all instances) + B × (number of instances where you hold the final best solution)

The bonus value B will be announced with the full competition rules.

The team with the highest total score wins in each track.

Leaderboards

Three public views will be maintained during the competition:

LeaderboardDescription
Per-instanceChronological evolution of the best participant solution for each instance (team, cost, timestamp).
Global rankingAggregate score of each team, updated in real time.
Hexaly comparisonFor each instance, the Hexaly baseline cost alongside the current best participant solution, with the percentage gap.

Solutions that set a new best are recorded but not published until the competition ends, preventing teams from locally improving each other's results.

Rules

No hardware restrictions. Teams may use any computational resources available to them.

Any software is allowed. Commercial solvers, open-source tools, or custom implementations. Hexaly provides free academic licenses to all registered participants.

SMIO membership required. Every participant must be a valid SMIO member. Through the ALIO alliance, members of other national societies can obtain SMIO membership at a discounted rate.

One team per person. Each person may belong to only one team.

Official submissions only. Solutions must be submitted through the web platform. No manual or email submissions.

Results are public. By participating, teams agree that their final solutions (but not their code) may be published as part of a post-competition benchmark library.
