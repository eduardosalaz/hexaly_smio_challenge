What is this Challenge?

The SMIO–Hexaly Location Routing Challenge is an optimization competition organized by the Mexican Society of Operations Research (SMIO) in collaboration with Hexaly. It is tied to the XIV Conference of SMIO (CSMIO 2026), to be held on October 7–9, 2026, at the Universidad de Monterrey, Nuevo León, Mexico.

Teams compete to find high-quality solutions to a set of Capacitated Location Routing Problem (CLRP) instances. Before the competition opens, Hexaly Optimizer produces a baseline solution for each instance. Participants then attempt to find the best solutions they can, using any method of their choice. A public leaderboard tracks progress in real time.

How It Works

1. Register and Prepare

Form a team, register on the platform, and develop your methods using the open-source instance generator (released in June 2026). The official competition instances won't be published until the competition starts.

2. Compete

When the competition window opens (~3 weeks in July), the official instances and Hexaly's baseline solutions are published. Submit your best solutions through the web platform. The leaderboard updates in real time.

3. Present at CSMIO

Winners are invited to present their approaches at a dedicated Challenge session during CSMIO 2026 in Monterrey. Results are published as a permanent benchmark.

The Problem: Location Routing

The CLRP combines two hard decisions: where to open depots and how to route vehicles from those depots to serve customers. It's directly relevant to companies like OXXO, Bimbo, and FEMSA, who face these decisions daily in Mexico's logistics landscape.

No single algorithmic paradigm dominates this problem, making it open to metaheuristics, decomposition methods, branch-and-price, matheuristics, machine learning, and hybrid approaches.
